## Collected Book 4 References

### From the Series Overview (`series-overview.md`)

- **Years:** 809–814 CE. Yusuf ages 39–44.
- **Central Question:** Can the next generation prove worthy — and can the old man let go?
- **Khalid ibn Yusuf** is the heir who must prove himself in Book 4, and his arc mirrors his father's — but where Yusuf built from nothing, Khalid must prove he can preserve and adapt what was built for him, which is in some ways harder.
- **Ja'far al-Barmaki's** relationship with Yusuf is the moral crux of Book 2 and the haunting of Books 3 and 4.
- **Harun al-Rashid's** relationship with Yusuf in Books 3 and 4 is that of a man with his favourite tool — valued, maintained, and always at risk of being discarded.
- **The Recurring Image (Dastan):** The final stage of the motif is reconciliation — Yusuf chooses to honour all commitments at personal cost, refusing to let the architecture of power destroy those who trusted him.

### From the CLAUDE.md Project Bible

- **POV Expansion:** Book 4 expands to include Khalid, Zahra, and Omar's perspectives in designated chapters (breaking the Yusuf-only POV of Books 1–3).
- **Yusuf's Arc — Release:** He must surrender control — the hardest thing he's ever done — and let Khalid prove himself.
- **Omar ibn Ali:** Fights to defend his neighbourhood during the siege in Book 4.
- **Khalid ibn Yusuf:** Deuteragonist of Book 4. Quiet, scholarly, deceptively passive. At ten, he asks: "If two men both claim to be Caliph, who is right?" — and his father has no answer.
- **The Cost of Ambition:** Book 4 costs Yusuf control — which turns out to be the price of redemption.
- **The Khorasan Gate tiles:** Ali's masterpiece. Survive the siege in Book 4. The endurance of physical craft vs. the fragility of financial systems.
- **Series Ending:** Happy — Khalid succeeds Yusuf, the House endures, but at significant personal cost. Not a fairy tale. A happy ending earned through sacrifice.

### Key Plot Beats (from CLAUDE.md summary)

1. Civil war. Yusuf serves al-Amin publicly while feeding intelligence to al-Ma'mun. The household prepares to flee.
2. The siege of Baghdad. Omar defends his neighbourhood. The Khorasan Gate survives.
3. Al-Amin falls. Yusuf's decision to honour all commitments from both sides — his first selfless act. Khalid proves himself to the new vizier.
4. The handover. Yusuf lets go.

### Key Historical Events (809–814 CE)

- **809 CE:** Harun al-Rashid dies (March). Al-Amin proclaimed Caliph. Revokes al-Ma'mun's succession rights.
- **810 CE:** Al-Amin revokes succession. Empire fractures.
- **811 CE:** Tahir ibn Husayn's army marches west. Khalid's education begins.
- **812 CE:** Siege of Baghdad begins.
- **813 CE (September):** Al-Amin killed. Al-Ma'mun proclaimed Caliph. Yusuf honours all obligations.
- **814 CE:** Khalid meets al-Fadl ibn Sahl (al-Ma'mun's vizier). The handover.

### Unresolved Threads That Must Pay Off in Book 4

- **Khalid's question** ("Who is right before the war is over?") (planted in Book 3) — series-ending answer.
- **The desert sand pouch** — opened for the first time in Book 3; its final significance here.
- **The Sogdian's ghost (Dastan)** — final stage: reconciliation. Yusuf becomes the man who refuses to let power destroy those who trusted him.

- **The tile fragment** — picked up during the siege when a tile falls from the Khorasan Gate. Kept on Yusuf's desk. His father's craft, broken, still beautiful.

### Khalid's Key Scene (from character bible)

**The Tutor Scene:** "If a merchant has ten dinars and lends five to a man who will repay six, and uses the other five to buy goods worth eight — what is his profit? ... Three dinars and the goodwill of the man who borrowed five. The goodwill is worth more than the dinar of interest, but only if the man is the right man. So the real question is: how do you know who the right man is?"
