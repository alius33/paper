## Part 1 — The Road (Ch 1-9)

By the end of the drafted chapters (Ch 1-3), Yusuf is 17 years old (787 CE). He has completed the five-day outbound journey to Kufa. Chapters 2 and 3 cover the full road journey: two khans, the ink merchant intelligence (3 vs 8 dirhams), Umar's backstory (scholar father turned clerk), the Banu Asad standoff and Layla identity fracture, Sa'dan's farewell (hobble cord with drover's hitch), and the approach to Kufa. Key relationships formed on the road: Sa'dan and Sayeed (departed south with Hajj pilgrims at Kufa gate, Ch 3 farewell), Umar deepened (his personal history earned through Yusuf's initiative, not volunteered). Key thematic threads established: "learning to see beneath the surface" (Ch 2), identity fracture — Yusuf is half merchant-system, half Bedouin-tribe (Ch 3), "trade is time" (Ch 3), Kufa as "a wound" (Ch 2-3). Yusuf's one act of agency: reporting the ink merchant intelligence to Umar, reframing it as market analysis.

## Cross-Chapter Continuity Notes

**Character naming:** Ustad Umar (journeyman/road teacher) and Omar al-Dimashqi (workshop head journeyman) are distinct characters. Both appear from Ch 1 onward but are never in the same scene. Omar is called "Omar" in workshop scenes; Umar is called "Umar" or "Ustad Umar" on the road and at the Kufa mosque.

**Yusuf's age:** 17 at story open (Ch 1). Turns 18 between Ch 4 and Ch 5 (Kufa in 787, suq mission ~Feb 788).

**The iron chest:** Introduced Ch 1 (iron-bound, leather straps, heavy lock, 12 sample sheets). Carried to khan storeroom in Ch 2 (corner near stables). Opened in Ch 3 khan (cotton wadding, 12 sheets). Lighter after Kufa sales (Ch 3 end). Set down in workshop courtyard on return (Ch 4 opening).

**Khorasan Gate tiles:** Ch 4 (rooftop scene) — amber in the last light, blue and green and gold, geometric interlocking. Ch 5 (final scene) — turquoise and cobalt. Minor colour description variance (amber vs turquoise); both include blue tones. Future chapters should reconcile to a consistent palette. Note: "turquoise and cobalt" is described as "the colours of a homeland he had never seen" — the Khorasani palette. The amber tone in Ch 4 may be the effect of sunset light on the tiles.

**Omar al-Dimashqi's tenure:** Ch 4 states he has been at workshop since age 12, for 15 years (making him ~27). This is consistent with his seniority and role.

**Sizing formula:** Ch 4 establishes Yusuf earns the secret formula (wheat starch and alum, with undisclosed proportions). Ch 5 references Yusuf having learned it 3 months prior.

**Sa'dan's animal:** Rides a mule (Ch 1, Ch 2). Sayeed rides a small donkey (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Umar's donkey:** First confirmed in Ch 2 (line 237: "riding his donkey" during the Banu Asad standoff scene). Not mentioned in Ch 1.

**Umar's family backstory:** Father's story told in Ch 2 (tea scene, Day 3 — scholar turned clerk, silk trader, fired, died). Uncle's story told in Ch 3 (Great Mosque — caravan attacked, walked barefoot, died at shrine). These are two separate revelations in two separate chapters.

**Abu Said's reed pen gesture:** Established in Ch 4 (line 65) as habitual — picks up dry reed pen, turns it between fingers. Recurs in Ch 5 (line 35) in the same form. Track as recurring character detail.

**The Caliph's Cream sheet count:** [OPEN QUESTION: exact sheet count arithmetic does not fully reconcile — track as ongoing continuity question.] 12 sample sheets departed Baghdad in the iron chest. 2 additional sheets carried on Yusuf's person (inside his robe) and given away (to Tariq, to the student). 24 on consignment to Abu Yaqub. Individual sales to scribes ("a few sheets here, a few there"). Abu Said's debrief in Ch 4 gives a total of 37 sheets sold. The 12 samples alone cannot account for 37 + 2 given away + an unknown number remaining in the chest at departure. The 24 consignment sheets and individual sales likely came from additional stock beyond the 12 samples (possibly from Ibn Zubair's bulk shipment or additional stock not described being loaded). The text does not explicitly resolve this.

**Mansur's customer tenure:** 8-year customer of Abu Said's workshop (Ch 5, line 81). Rashid ibn Musa also has an 8-year relationship with Abu Said (Ch 5, line 201).

**"Scholar's Cream" name:** Improvised by Yusuf during conversation with Abu Yaqub in Ch 3 (line 383) as a sales tactic. Not an official rename — Yusuf attributes the alternative name to his master, though Abu Said never actually said this. Track whether this name recurs or becomes official.
