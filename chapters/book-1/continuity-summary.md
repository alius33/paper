## Part 1 — The Road (Ch 1-9)

By the end of the drafted chapters (Ch 1-4), Yusuf is 17 years old (787 CE). He has completed the five-day outbound journey to Kufa and spent three days in the city. Chapters 2-3 cover the full road journey: two khans, the ink merchant intelligence (3 vs 8 dirhams), Umar's backstory (scholar father turned clerk), the Banu Asad standoff and Layla identity fracture, Sa'dan's farewell (hobble cord with drover's hitch), and the approach to Kufa. Chapter 4 covers three days in Kufa: Sa'dan and Sayeed's departure south, Kufa's Great Mosque (Umar's uncle revelation — the second personal crack after the father story in Ch 2), Tariq the bread thief, five failed paper sales, the khan at night (sarrafs with coin-clipping explanation, mudaraba argument), the "Scholar's Cream" improvisation with Abu Yaqub (24 standard-grade sheets on consignment), and the stealth thesis: "The people who valued it could not afford it. The people who could afford it did not value it." Key relationships formed: Sa'dan and Sayeed (departed south, Ch 4 farewell), Umar deepened (father story Ch 2, uncle story Ch 4), Tariq (barefoot grammar student who reframes what paper means). Key thematic threads: "learning to see beneath the surface" (Ch 2), identity fracture — Yusuf is half merchant-system, half Bedouin-tribe (Ch 3, echoed in Ch 4 reflection), "trade is time" (Ch 3), Kufa as "a wound" (Ch 2-4), trust as currency (sarraf motif, mudaraba, Ch 4), "hold many stories at once" (Ch 4). Iron chest contents clarified: 12 Caliph's Cream samples + 40 standard-grade sheets. Acts of agency: reporting ink intelligence to Umar (Ch 2), improvising the "Scholar's Cream" rebranding (Ch 4), giving two Caliph's Cream sheets to Tariq and a student (Ch 4).

## Cross-Chapter Continuity Notes

**Character naming:** Ustad Umar (journeyman/road teacher) and Omar al-Dimashqi (workshop head journeyman) are distinct characters. Both appear from Ch 1 onward but are never in the same scene. Omar is called "Omar" in workshop scenes; Umar is called "Umar" or "Ustad Umar" on the road and at the Kufa mosque.

**Yusuf's age:** 17 at story open (Ch 1). Turns 18 between Ch 4 and Ch 5 (Kufa in 787, suq mission ~Feb 788).

**The iron chest:** Introduced Ch 1 (iron-bound, leather straps, heavy lock, 12 sample sheets). Carried to khan storeroom in Ch 2 (corner near stables). Opened in Ch 4 khan — two tiers: 12 Caliph's Cream in cotton wadding (top) + 40 standard-grade sheets (below). Lighter after Kufa sales (Ch 4 end). Set down in workshop courtyard on return (Ch 5 opening).

**Khorasan Gate tiles:** Ch 4 (rooftop scene) — amber in the last light, blue and green and gold, geometric interlocking. Ch 5 (final scene) — turquoise and cobalt. Minor colour description variance (amber vs turquoise); both include blue tones. Future chapters should reconcile to a consistent palette. Note: "turquoise and cobalt" is described as "the colours of a homeland he had never seen" — the Khorasani palette. The amber tone in Ch 4 may be the effect of sunset light on the tiles.

**Omar al-Dimashqi's tenure:** Ch 4 states he has been at workshop since age 12, for 15 years (making him ~27). This is consistent with his seniority and role.

**Sizing formula:** Ch 4 establishes Yusuf earns the secret formula (wheat starch and alum, with undisclosed proportions). Ch 5 references Yusuf having learned it 3 months prior.

**Sa'dan's animal:** Rides a mule (Ch 1, Ch 2). Sayeed rides a small donkey (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Umar's donkey:** First confirmed in Ch 2 (line 237: "riding his donkey" during the Banu Asad standoff scene). Not mentioned in Ch 1.

**Umar's family backstory:** Father's story told in Ch 2 (tea scene, Day 3 — scholar turned clerk, silk trader, fired, died). Uncle's story told in Ch 4 (Great Mosque — caravan attacked, walked barefoot, died at shrine). These are two separate revelations in two separate chapters. Ch 4 explicitly marks the uncle as the SECOND crack in Umar's wall.

**Abu Said's reed pen gesture:** Established in Ch 4 (line 65) as habitual — picks up dry reed pen, turns it between fingers. Recurs in Ch 5 (line 35) in the same form. Track as recurring character detail.

**The Caliph's Cream sheet count:** [RESOLVED in Ch 4 revised draft.] Iron chest contains two tiers: 12 Caliph's Cream samples in cotton wadding (top) + 40 standard-grade workshop paper (below). 2 CC sheets carried on Yusuf's person and given away (to Tariq, to the student). 24 standard-grade sheets on consignment to Abu Yaqub. Additional individual sales of standard-grade to scribes (~13 sheets). Total standard-grade sold: ~37 (matching Ch 5 debrief). The 12 CC samples were shown but not sold — they remain in the chest. Arithmetic now reconciles: 40 standard - 24 consignment - ~13 individual = ~3 remaining standard + 12 CC = chest "lighter but not empty."

**Mansur's customer tenure:** 8-year customer of Abu Said's workshop (Ch 5, line 81). Rashid ibn Musa also has an 8-year relationship with Abu Said (Ch 5, line 201).

**"Scholar's Cream" name:** Improvised by Yusuf during conversation with Abu Yaqub in Ch 4 as a sales tactic. Not an official rename — Yusuf attributes the alternative name to his master, though Abu Said never actually said this. Track whether this name recurs or becomes official.

**Sa'dan's hobble cord:** Given to Yusuf in Ch 3 (farewell campfire scene). Worn on Yusuf's wrist. Sa'dan notices it in Ch 4 farewell ("Keep it tight. It loosens in the heat."). Yusuf turns it at the Ch 4 departure — tactile anchor, replacing generic fidgeting. Track forward as recurring personal object.
