## Relationship Notes

**Relationships not yet active in Book 1:**
- **Yusuf -- Zahra bint Abbas:** Not introduced until Book 2 (~795 CE). Her absence in Book 1 is deliberate -- Yusuf's emotional world is defined by male relationships (father, brother, master, friends) and by Layla's memory.
- **Yusuf -- Ishaq bar Yohannan:** Recruited in Ch 35 (Part 4). Their first meeting "crackles -- two analytical minds sizing each other up." Ishaq is sardonic, brilliant, and privately lonely. He sees in Yusuf's offer a seat at the table, not just a chair in the anteroom. By the end of Book 1, they have a working partnership but the deep friendship (and its eventual fracture over the Barmakid Gamble) belongs to Books 2-3.
- **Yusuf -- Yonan al-Nasrani:** Guide for the Constantinople venture (Chs 38-42). Sardonic, worldly, belongs to both the Islamic and Byzantine worlds. Their dynamic shifts from employer/guide to mutual respect. Yonan stays in the borderlands; Yusuf returns to Baghdad. A brief, understated farewell.
- **Yusuf -- Brother Michael:** Encountered at the Stoudios Monastery in Constantinople (Ch 40-41). A Nestorian monk who becomes a key contact for cross-civilisational trade.
- **Yusuf -- Demetrios of Chios:** The Byzantine merchant-banker who teaches Yusuf the three pillars (Capital, Information, Influence) in Ch 41. A single transformative encounter.

**Key dynamic to track across Books 2-4:**
The pattern of Yusuf's relationships follows a consistent arc: genuine connection, then the connection tested by Yusuf's ambition, then a reckoning. Abu Said's rupture (Book 1) is the template. Omar al-Dimashqi's cooling respect, Ishaq's moral crisis, Musa's clear-eyed unease, and eventually Zahra's grief -- each relationship follows this shape, with variations. The tracker should flag when each relationship enters its "tested" phase.
