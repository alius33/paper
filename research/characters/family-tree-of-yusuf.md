# The Family Tree of Yusuf ibn Ali

### First Generation: The Great-Great-Great-Grandfather

**Name:** Amr ibn Ka\'b al-Asadi

- **Born:** c. 600 CE (Banu Asad tribe, near Yathrib/Medina)

- **Died:** c. 660 CE (near Medina)

- **Spouse:** Amina bint Khalid (of the Banu Asad)

  - **Backstory:** A pious and steadfast woman who was among the early female converts alongside her husband. She was known for her generosity and for managing their small but prosperous date farm while Amr was on campaign.

- **Children:** **Zayd ibn Amr** (born 630 CE), one other son, two daughters.

- **Key Events & Notes:**

  - Was a young man when the Prophet Muhammad arrived in Medina (622 CE).

  - As a member of the Banu Asad tribe located near the city, he was among the early converts to Islam.

  - Fought alongside the Prophet and his companions in several key battles for the nascent Muslim community.

  - Lived his life as a respected elder in the Medina community, known as one of the *Ansar* (helpers). Witnessed the reigns of the first three Rashidun Caliphs.

### Second Generation: The Great-Great-Grandfather

**Name:** Zayd ibn Amr al-Asadi

- **Born:** 630 CE (near Medina)

- **Died:** 705 CE (near Damascus)

- **Spouse:** Samira bint Malik (of the Banu Asad)

  - **Backstory:** A resilient desert woman who accompanied Zayd on his campaigns and managed their new estate in Syria with practicality and strength. She ensured her children retained their Arabic language and tribal customs despite being raised in a new land.

- **Children:** Harith ibn Zayd (born 658 CE), two other sons, one daughter.

- **Key Events & Notes:**

  - Fought as a young warrior under the Rashidun Caliph Umar, participating in the conquest of Syria.

  - Was granted a plot of land outside Damascus as a reward for his service.

  - Witnessed the entire First Fitna (civil war) and the rise of the Umayyad Caliphate under Mu\'awiya I.

  - Remained a landowner, transitioning from a life of a warrior to that of a patriarch on his estate.

### Third Generation: The Great-Grandfather

**Name:** Harith ibn Zayd al-Asadi

- **Born:** 658 CE (on the family estate near Damascus)

- **Died:** 735 CE (in Merv, Khorasan)

- **Spouse:** Asma al-Ghassani

  - **Backstory:** A woman from a notable local Arab family in Syria that had Christian Ghassanid roots before converting to Islam. She was educated and brought a level of Syrian sophistication to the family, though she always felt Harith\'s heart was elsewhere.

- **Children:** Suleiman ibn Harith (born 690 CE), one other son.

- **Key Events & Notes:**

  - Born during the Caliphate of Ali ibn Abi Talib. Was 3 when Ali was assassinated.

  - Grew up in the heartland of Umayyad power, witnessing their consolidation of the empire.

  - Was 22 during the Battle of Karbala (680 CE). The event, and the Umayyad response, deeply disturbed him, cementing his family\'s private sympathy for the Alids.

  - In 705 CE, after his father\'s death and a dispute with a local Umayyad official over land taxes, he moved his family east to the booming city of Merv in Khorasan, seeking opportunity and distance from the Umayyad court.

### Fourth Generation: The Grandfather

**Name:** Suleiman ibn Harith al-Asadi

- **Born:** 690 CE (near Damascus)

- **Died:** 760 CE (in Baghdad)

- **Spouse:** Zainab bint Abdullah

  - **Backstory:** The daughter of another Arab merchant family that had migrated to Merv. She was known for her piety and was a strong advocate for her husband\'s convictions, fully supporting the decision to name their son Ali.

- **Children:** **Ali ibn Suleiman** (Yusuf\'s father, born 720 CE), two daughters.

- **Key Events & Notes:**

  - Was 15 years old when the family migrated to Merv.

  - Was raised on his father\'s stories of the Umayyad\'s rise and the tragedy of Husayn.

  - Thrived in the multicultural environment of Merv, becoming a successful merchant dealing in textiles and spices.

  - Deeply respected the piety of the Alid family line. He named his son \"Ali\" as a statement of this respect and a quiet protest against the memory of the Umayyads.

  - Lived to see the Abbasid Revolution succeed and moved with his son\'s family to the new city of Baghdad in his final years.

### Fifth Generation: The Father

**Name:** Ali ibn Suleiman

- **Born:** 720 CE (in Merv, Khorasan)

- **Died:** 788 CE (in Baghdad)

- **First Spouse:** Fatima bint Farrokh (A Khorasani noblewoman)

  - **Children:** Omar ibn Ali, Bakr ibn Ali.

- **Second Spouse:** (Died without issue)

- **Third Spouse:** Layla bint Jabir (of a Bedouin tribe near Mecca)

  - **Children:** **Yusuf ibn Ali** (born 770 CE)

- **Key Events & Notes:**

  - Became a master tile-maker in Merv.

  - Joined the Abbasid cause, seeing it as a righteous movement.

  - Migrated to Baghdad to help build the new capital for Caliph al-Mansur.

  - Was rewarded with a house inside the city walls near the Khorasan Gate and land outside the city.

### Sixth Generation: The Main Character

**Name:** Yusuf ibn Ali

- **Born:** 770 CE (in Baghdad)

- **Mother:** Layla bint Jabir

- **Half-Brothers:** Omar and Bakr

- **Notes:** Raised in a divided household, learning the Khorasani ways from his father\'s world and the Bedouin ways from his mother\'s stories. His unique upbringing and position by the Khorasan Gate gave him an unparalleled education in the ways of the world.
